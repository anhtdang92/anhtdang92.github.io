🎯 Problem

Health data from Apple Watch and iPhone stays siloed in HealthKit — users see raw numbers (heart rate, steps, sleep) but get no contextual analysis. A resting heart rate of 72 BPM means nothing without knowing your trend, your sleep quality, or your recent activity level.

I wanted to build an agentic system that interprets biometric patterns across multiple data streams and delivers actionable health insights, all processed entirely on-device for privacy.

🔧 Approach

Built a native Swift iOS app integrating with HealthKit APIs to pull real-time biometric data — heart rate, HRV, steps, sleep stages, blood oxygen. The app monitors these streams continuously and builds a rolling profile of the user's health baselines.

Implemented on-device ML models using Core ML to analyze trends and detect anomalies. The agentic layer synthesizes multiple data streams into personalized health recommendations — for example, detecting that elevated resting heart rate combined with poor sleep quality suggests overtraining.

🧠 Key Decisions

Chose on-device inference over cloud APIs to keep sensitive health data private — no data leaves the phone. Health data is among the most sensitive personal information, and users shouldn't have to trust a third-party server with their biometrics.

Used Core ML for model deployment rather than TensorFlow Lite for tighter iOS integration. Core ML integrates natively with the Apple Neural Engine and GPU, providing better performance and battery efficiency on Apple silicon.

Designed the agentic architecture to reason across multiple biometric signals rather than analyzing each in isolation. Heart rate, sleep, activity, and HRV are interconnected — an elevated heart rate means different things depending on whether you just exercised, slept poorly, or are getting sick.
